Activity 1: Rooftop Solar PV System (5 kW)

System Explanation

This rooftop solar PV installation is sized at 5 kW and integrates a grid-tied inverter and a battery backup system. It is designed to supply household or small-institutional loads with priority to on-site consumption, battery charging, and export of surplus energy to the utility network.

Source of Energy

Primary source: sunlight. Solar photovoltaic modules convert incident solar radiation into direct current (DC) electricity using semiconductor cells.

Conversion Process

DC power from PV modules is routed to an inverter which converts it to AC suitable for standard appliances. Excess energy charges the battery storage; when batteries are full, surplus can be exported to the grid. A charge controller / PV combiner ensures safe operation.

Output/Utilization

The system supplies AC electricity for lighting, fans, home appliances, and small electronic loads. Battery storage provides backup during low-sun periods and nighttime.

Real-World Application

Rooftop PV systems reduce household energy bills, lower carbon footprint, and support grid resilience. They are ideal for urban and peri-urban residential or commercial rooftops seeking reliable renewable energy.

Activity 2: Hybrid Solar PV & Wind System for Rural Health Clinic

System Explanation

This hybrid system combines solar PV and a wind turbine, coordinated by a charge controller and a battery bank. It is tailored to power essential equipment at a rural health clinic, prioritizing continuous, reliable supply for critical loads.

Source of Energy

Energy sources are sunlight (solar PV) and wind (turbine). Combining both improves generation reliability across diurnal and seasonal variations.

Conversion Process

Solar panels produce DC; wind turbines typically generate AC. Both inputs are managed via a charge controller (or rectifier + controller) to charge batteries. An inverter supplies regulated AC to clinic equipment such as lighting, refrigeration, and diagnostics.

Output/Utilization

The system supports vaccine refrigerators, lighting, diagnostic devices, and communication tools necessary for uninterrupted healthcare services in remote settings.

Real-World Application

Hybrid renewables reduce reliance on diesel gensets, lower operating costs, and ensure 24×7 power for medical services, improving healthcare outcomes in off-grid rural communities.

Activity 3: Solar-Powered Irrigation Pumping System (Off-grid)

System Explanation

This solar irrigation system powers an electric motor pump using PV-generated electricity. It is designed for off-grid agricultural applications, delivering water for crop irrigation and livestock needs without diesel or grid dependency.

Source of Energy

Source: solar radiation captured by photovoltaic panels mounted near the pump site or on-farm arrays.

Conversion Process

PV modules produce DC; a controller (often an MPPT) optimizes power and supplies the motor drive. In some systems an inverter or a DC motor is used depending on design; mechanical energy from the motor lifts water from wells or canals.

Output/Utilization

The pumped water is used for crop irrigation, greenhouse watering, and livestock supply. Systems can operate directly during peak sun or use battery/storage for extended operation.

Real-World Application

Solar pumps reduce fuel costs, lower emissions, and increase irrigation reliability in rural areas. They support agricultural productivity and food security, especially where grid supply is absent or unreliable.
